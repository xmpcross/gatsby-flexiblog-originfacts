## Best Times to Visit for Value

Spring (late March to early April) and autumn (October to November) are Tokyo's peak tourist seasons, coinciding with cherry blossom and autumn foliage respectively. Prices for accommodation spike significantly during golden week (late April to early May) and cherry blossom peak, so book several months in advance or adjust your dates slightly to avoid the premium.

January and February are the quietest months and the best for value, with accommodation prices at their lowest and attractions far less crowded. Summer (July to August) is hot, humid, and busy, but mid-week travel in August can yield good deals as domestic travellers cluster around school holiday weekends.

If your schedule is flexible, travelling on weekdays rather than weekends in any season makes a noticeable difference, both in crowd levels and, at some attractions, entry pricing.
