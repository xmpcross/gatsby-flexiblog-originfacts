## 10 Unique Things to Do in Sydney

**1. Walk the Bondi to Coogee Coastal Path**

The Bondi to Coogee walk is one of the great free experiences in the Southern Hemisphere, and somehow it still doesn't get the attention it deserves outside Australia. The 6-kilometre trail hugs dramatic sandstone headlands, passes through quiet coves and cemetery parks, and delivers views of the Pacific that stop you mid-step. Allow two to three hours, start early to beat the crowds, and finish with a swim at Coogee Beach before catching the bus back. Bring water and sunscreen â€” the path offers very little shade.

**2. Explore the Rock Pools at Mahon Pool**

Mahon Pool in Maroubra is Sydney's best-kept ocean pool secret. Carved into the rocky coastline and constantly refreshed by ocean swells, it feels wilder and more dramatic than the more famous Bondi Icebergs. The surrounding rocks are flat and excellent for sitting, the water is clear, and the crowds are minimal even on warm weekends. It's the kind of place locals have been quietly keeping to themselves for years â€” arrive before 9am for a genuinely serene swim.

**3. Catch the Ferry to Manly and Walk the Spit to Manly Trail**

The Manly Ferry from Circular Quay is itself one of the world's great commutes, offering uninterrupted harbour views for thirty minutes. But most visitors walk Manly's promenade and call it done. The better move is to take a bus to the Spit Bridge and walk the 10-kilometre Spit to Manly trail back through Sydney Harbour National Park. The path winds through bush land, past hidden beaches, and over rocky headlands with views that most tourists never see. End the walk at Manly Beach and reward yourself with fish and chips on the sand.

It's a genuinely world-class gallery experience that most Sydney visitors walk straight past.

**5. Eat Your Way Through Spice Alley in Kensington**

Spice Alley is Sydney's answer to the hawker centres of Southeast Asia â€” a laneway lined with timber stalls serving Singaporean, Malaysian, Indonesian, and Thai food at prices that make restaurant dining feel extravagant by comparison. It's casual, communal, and consistently excellent. Order the laksa, the char kway teow, or the satay plates, grab a cold beer from the bar in the middle, and settle in at the shared tables for the evening. It's an atmosphere that rewards unhurried eating.

**6. Take a Sunrise Swim at Icebergs or Giles Baths**

Sydney's ocean pools are institutions, and experiencing one at sunrise is an entirely different proposition from an afternoon visit.
